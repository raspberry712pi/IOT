<>Aim:- Displaying Time over 16x2 LCD Display using Raspberry Pi.
<>Requirements:- Raspberry Pi,micro SD Card,16x2 LCD with header pins,Breadboard,jumper wires.
<>Assembling the 16x2 LCD

1.First,snap the header pins,so you have 1 line of 16.
2.Place the header pins up through the holes of the display. The short side of the header pins should stick up.
3.Now using a hot soldering iron and same solder, slowly solder each of the pins.
4.Its now ready for use.

<>Connections

VSS (Pin 1 on LCD) to GND on Raspberry Pi
VDD (Pin 2 on LCD) to 5V on Raspberry Pi
VO (Pin 3 on LCD) to the middle pin of the potentiometer
RS (Pin 4 on LCD) to GPIO 22 (Pin 15) on Raspberry Pi
RW (Pin 5 on LCD) to GND (for write mode)
E (Pin 6 on LCD) to GPIO 18 (Pin 12) on Raspberry Pi
D4 (Pin 11 on LCD) to GPIO 16 (Pin 36) on Raspberry Pi
D5 (Pin 12 on LCD) to GPIO 11 (Pin 23) on Raspberry Pi
D6 (Pin 13 on LCD) to GPIO 12 (Pin 32) on Raspberry Pi
D7 (Pin 14 on LCD) to GPIO 15 (Pin 10) on Raspberry Pi
A (Pin 15 on LCD) to 5V (if your LCD has a backlight)
K (Pin 16 on LCD) to GND (if your LCD has a backlight)

<>Enable I2C via sudo raspi-config>Interfacing options>I2C enable
